## ⚙️ Problem Domains

These problems represent increasing levels of complexity in power system optimization, ranging from basic cost minimization to realistic operational constraints and multi-objective scenarios.

**Economic Dispatch (ED)**
Determines the optimal power output of generators to minimize total fuel cost while satisfying system demand and operational constraints.

**Economic Emission Dispatch (EED)**
Extends ED by incorporating emission objectives, aiming to balance fuel cost and environmental impact through multi-objective optimization.

**Unit Commitment (UC)**
Involves scheduling the ON/OFF status of generators over a time horizon to meet demand at minimum cost while considering startup and shutdown constraints.

**Valve-Point Effects (VPE)**
Models non-smooth and non-convex cost characteristics caused by turbine valve operations, increasing the realism and difficulty of the optimization problem.

**Prohibited Operating Zones (POZ)**
Represents restricted operating regions where generators cannot operate due to physical or operational limitations, introducing discontinuities in the solution space.
